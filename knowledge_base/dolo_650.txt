OVERVIEW

Dolo 650 is a brand name for paracetamol, also known as acetaminophen, which is one of the most commonly used medicines worldwide for relieving pain and reducing fever. It belongs to the class of medications known as analgesics and antipyretics. Unlike nonsteroidal anti inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), paracetamol has very little anti inflammatory activity, making it primarily useful for pain relief and fever reduction rather than treating inflammation. Each Dolo 650 tablet contains 650 mg of paracetamol as its active ingredient. The medicine is widely prescribed because it is generally effective, well tolerated, and suitable for a broad range of patients when used at recommended doses. It is commonly available over the counter in many countries, although higher strength formulations may require medical advice depending on local regulations. The exact mechanism of action is not completely understood, but paracetamol is believed to work primarily by inhibiting prostaglandin synthesis within the central nervous system. This inhibition reduces the brain's perception of pain and lowers the hypothalamic temperature regulating center, thereby reducing fever. Unlike NSAIDs, paracetamol has minimal effects on peripheral cyclooxygenase enzymes, which explains why it does not significantly reduce swelling or inflammation. The medication is rapidly absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract after oral administration, with pain relief usually beginning within 30 to 60 minutes. Its effects generally last for four to six hours, depending on the individual and the severity of symptoms. Paracetamol is extensively metabolized in the liver, and most of its metabolites are eliminated through the kidneys in the urine. A small portion of the drug is converted into a potentially toxic metabolite called N acetyl p benzoquinone imine, which is normally detoxified by glutathione in the liver. However, excessive doses can overwhelm this protective mechanism and cause severe liver damage. Because of its favorable safety profile at therapeutic doses, Dolo 650 is frequently recommended as a first line treatment for mild to moderate pain and fever.

INDICATIONS & USES

Dolo 650 is primarily indicated for the treatment of fever associated with viral, bacterial, or inflammatory illnesses. It is commonly prescribed to reduce elevated body temperature in conditions such as influenza, the common cold, COVID 19, dengue, and other infectious diseases. The medicine is also widely used for the relief of mild to moderate pain. It is effective in treating headaches, including tension headaches and mild migraine attacks. Dental pain resulting from tooth infections, dental procedures, or tooth extraction can also be managed with paracetamol. It is frequently used for muscle aches, body pain, and joint discomfort that are not primarily caused by inflammatory conditions. Dolo 650 is commonly recommended for pain following minor surgical procedures and for recovery after certain medical interventions. It is also useful for relieving menstrual cramps in many women. Patients experiencing back pain, neck pain, or soft tissue injuries may receive paracetamol either alone or in combination with other therapies. The medicine is often used in osteoarthritis to reduce pain and improve daily functioning, although it does not slow disease progression. In children, lower strength formulations of paracetamol are commonly used to manage fever and pain under appropriate dosing guidelines. Physicians frequently recommend paracetamol for patients who cannot safely take NSAIDs because of stomach ulcers, bleeding disorders, kidney disease, or allergies to NSAIDs. Off label uses include supportive management of fever and discomfort in various viral illnesses, postoperative pain management, and pain associated with vaccination. It is often included in combination products for cough and cold symptoms, although patients must be careful to avoid taking multiple products containing paracetamol simultaneously. Healthcare providers may also use paracetamol as part of multimodal pain management strategies alongside other medications to reduce the need for stronger pain relievers such as opioids.

DOSAGE & ADMINISTRATION

The dosage of Dolo 650 should always be individualized according to the patient's age, body weight, medical condition, and healthcare provider's recommendations. For most healthy adults, the usual dose is one 650 mg tablet every four to six hours as needed for pain or fever. Some individuals may require dosing every six to eight hours depending on symptom severity and response to treatment. The total daily dose should generally not exceed 3000 to 4000 mg of paracetamol from all sources in adults, although many healthcare professionals recommend remaining below 3000 mg daily whenever possible to reduce the risk of liver injury. Patients with liver disease, chronic alcohol use, malnutrition, or advanced age may require lower maximum daily doses. Dolo 650 should be swallowed whole with a full glass of water and should not be crushed unless advised by a healthcare provider. The medicine may be taken with or without food, although taking it with food may help reduce stomach discomfort in sensitive individuals. It can be taken at any time of the day according to symptom onset, provided that the recommended interval between doses is maintained. Patients should avoid taking multiple paracetamol containing products simultaneously because this significantly increases the risk of accidental overdose. If a dose is missed and the medication is being taken on a scheduled basis, it should be taken as soon as remembered unless it is almost time for the next scheduled dose. Patients should never take two doses together or take extra tablets to compensate for a missed dose. Adequate hydration should be maintained, especially when the medication is being used for fever. Individuals with severe kidney impairment may require longer intervals between doses under medical supervision. If pain persists for more than a few days or fever continues beyond three days despite treatment, medical evaluation is recommended to identify the underlying cause rather than continuing self medication indefinitely.

SIDE EFFECTS

Most people tolerate Dolo 650 well when it is taken at recommended doses, and serious adverse effects are relatively uncommon. Mild side effects may include nausea, mild stomach discomfort, loss of appetite, or occasional vomiting. Some individuals may experience mild dizziness, headache, or fatigue after taking the medication, although these symptoms are generally uncommon. Rarely, patients may develop mild skin rashes or itching due to hypersensitivity reactions. Moderate side effects can include persistent nausea, abdominal pain, unusual weakness, dark colored urine, or yellowing of the skin and eyes, which may indicate liver injury and require immediate medical evaluation. Some patients may develop elevated liver enzymes that are detectable on blood tests before symptoms become apparent. Allergic reactions may present with swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat and should be treated as medical emergencies. Very rarely, serious skin reactions such as Stevens Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, or acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis have been reported. These conditions typically begin with fever, sore throat, or flu like symptoms followed by widespread rash, blistering, or peeling of the skin. Blood related complications such as thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, or pancytopenia are extremely rare but have been documented in some patients. The most serious adverse effect associated with paracetamol is acute liver failure resulting from overdose or prolonged excessive use. Symptoms of overdose may initially include nausea, vomiting, sweating, and abdominal discomfort before progressing to severe liver damage over the following days. In severe poisoning, patients may develop confusion, jaundice, bleeding abnormalities, kidney failure, and coma. Prompt medical treatment is essential because early administration of the antidote, N acetylcysteine, can significantly reduce the risk of permanent liver injury if given within the recommended time window. Patients should seek immediate medical attention whenever severe allergic reactions, breathing difficulty, extensive skin rash, persistent vomiting, or signs of liver damage occur.

WARNINGS & PRECAUTIONS

Dolo 650 should always be used according to the recommended dosage because exceeding the maximum daily limit significantly increases the risk of potentially life threatening liver damage. Individuals with known hypersensitivity to paracetamol or any component of the formulation should not take this medication. Patients with severe liver disease, chronic hepatitis, cirrhosis, or impaired liver function should consult a healthcare provider before using Dolo 650, as dose adjustments or alternative treatments may be necessary. Chronic alcohol consumption substantially increases the risk of liver toxicity because alcohol affects the metabolism of paracetamol and increases the formation of toxic metabolites. Patients are advised to avoid or minimize alcohol intake while taking this medicine. Individuals with severe kidney disease should use paracetamol cautiously and may require extended dosing intervals under medical supervision. Care should also be taken in patients who are malnourished, dehydrated, or have low glutathione reserves because they may be more susceptible to toxicity. Pregnant women can generally use paracetamol when clinically necessary and at the lowest effective dose for the shortest possible duration, as it is considered one of the preferred pain relievers during pregnancy under medical guidance. Paracetamol is also considered compatible with breastfeeding because only very small amounts pass into breast milk and significant adverse effects in nursing infants are uncommon. Patients should carefully read labels of all prescription and over the counter medicines to avoid unintentionally taking multiple products containing paracetamol. Certain medications that affect liver enzymes, including some anticonvulsants and tuberculosis treatments, may alter paracetamol metabolism and should be discussed with a healthcare provider. Individuals taking warfarin for prolonged periods should use paracetamol cautiously because regular long term use may enhance the anticoagulant effect and increase bleeding risk. Emergency medical attention should be sought immediately after any suspected overdose, even if no symptoms are initially present, because serious liver injury may develop several hours later despite the patient appearing well. Patients should also seek urgent medical care if they experience severe allergic reactions, difficulty breathing, persistent vomiting, yellowing of the skin or eyes, confusion, unusual bleeding, or any symptoms suggesting significant liver dysfunction.
