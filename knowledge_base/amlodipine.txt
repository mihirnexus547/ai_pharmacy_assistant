OVERVIEW

Amlodipine is a long acting calcium channel blocker that belongs to the dihydropyridine class of antihypertensive medications. It is widely prescribed to treat high blood pressure and certain forms of chest pain because of its proven effectiveness, favorable safety profile, and convenient once daily dosing schedule. The drug works primarily by relaxing the smooth muscles that line the walls of arteries, allowing blood vessels to widen and reducing resistance to blood flow. This vasodilation lowers blood pressure and decreases the workload placed on the heart during each heartbeat. Amlodipine selectively blocks L type calcium channels in vascular smooth muscle cells, preventing calcium ions from entering these cells, which is necessary for muscle contraction. Because the arteries remain more relaxed, blood can circulate more easily throughout the body, improving oxygen delivery to tissues and reducing cardiac strain. Unlike some other calcium channel blockers, amlodipine has relatively little direct effect on the electrical conduction system of the heart, making it less likely to significantly slow heart rate or impair cardiac conduction. It has a gradual onset of action and a prolonged elimination half life of approximately thirty to fifty hours, allowing blood pressure control to remain stable over a full twenty four hour period with a single daily dose. Amlodipine is extensively metabolized in the liver and is eliminated primarily as inactive metabolites through the kidneys. The medication is available as oral tablets in several strengths and is often included in combination products with other antihypertensive medications such as angiotensin receptor blockers, angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors, or cholesterol lowering drugs. Long term use has been shown to reduce cardiovascular risk by helping control persistent hypertension and improving coronary blood flow. Because of its long duration of action, it provides consistent therapeutic effects while minimizing fluctuations in blood pressure. It is commonly chosen as a first line treatment for hypertension in many clinical practice guidelines because it is effective across a wide range of patient populations, including older adults and individuals of diverse ethnic backgrounds. The medication does not cure hypertension or coronary artery disease, but regular use helps manage these chronic conditions and lowers the risk of complications such as stroke, heart attack, and heart failure.

INDICATIONS & USES

Amlodipine is primarily approved for the treatment of hypertension in adults and children of appropriate age to help lower elevated blood pressure and reduce the risk of cardiovascular complications. Effective blood pressure control decreases the likelihood of stroke, myocardial infarction, kidney disease progression, and other long term consequences of uncontrolled hypertension. The medication is also approved for the treatment of chronic stable angina, a condition in which reduced blood flow to the heart causes chest discomfort during physical activity or emotional stress. It is indicated for vasospastic angina, also known as Prinzmetal angina, where coronary artery spasms temporarily reduce blood flow to the heart muscle. By relaxing coronary arteries, amlodipine improves oxygen delivery to the heart and reduces episodes of chest pain. It may be used alone or in combination with other medications such as beta blockers, angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers, diuretics, or nitrates depending on the patient's cardiovascular condition. In many patients, combination therapy provides better blood pressure control than a single medication alone. Common off label uses include management of certain patients with coronary artery disease who require improved coronary blood flow despite not having classic angina symptoms. Some healthcare providers prescribe it to reduce symptoms associated with certain vascular disorders such as Raynaud phenomenon by improving circulation to the fingers and toes. It may also be used as part of a comprehensive treatment plan in patients with chronic kidney disease who require careful blood pressure management to slow disease progression. In patients with diabetes and hypertension, amlodipine contributes to reducing cardiovascular risk when blood pressure goals are achieved. It is frequently selected for older adults because of its sustained blood pressure lowering effects and generally good tolerability. The medication may also be considered in patients who cannot tolerate certain other classes of blood pressure medications because of cough, electrolyte disturbances, or other adverse effects. Although amlodipine effectively controls symptoms and blood pressure, it should always be used alongside lifestyle measures such as dietary improvement, sodium reduction, regular physical activity, smoking cessation, and weight management to maximize cardiovascular benefits.

DOSAGE & ADMINISTRATION

Amlodipine is administered orally once daily and can be taken at any consistent time each day to maintain stable drug levels in the bloodstream. It may be taken with food or on an empty stomach because food does not significantly affect its absorption or overall effectiveness. For most adults with hypertension, the usual starting dose is 5 milligrams once daily. Some patients, including older adults, individuals with low body weight, or those with significant liver impairment, may begin treatment with a lower starting dose of 2.5 milligrams once daily to reduce the likelihood of excessive blood pressure reduction. Depending on the patient's clinical response and blood pressure goals, the dose may be increased gradually after several weeks of therapy. The typical maintenance dose ranges from 5 to 10 milligrams once daily, with 10 milligrams representing the maximum recommended daily dose for most adults. For chronic stable angina or vasospastic angina, similar dosing recommendations are generally used, with gradual adjustment according to symptom control and tolerance. Because amlodipine has a long half life, patients should continue taking it every day even if they feel well, since hypertension often produces no noticeable symptoms. Blood pressure should be monitored regularly to evaluate treatment effectiveness and determine whether dose adjustments are necessary. Patients should swallow the tablet whole with water and avoid altering the prescribed dose without consulting their healthcare provider. If a dose is missed, it should be taken as soon as it is remembered unless it is almost time for the next scheduled dose. If the next dose is approaching, the missed dose should be skipped and the normal dosing schedule resumed. Patients should never take two doses at the same time to compensate for a missed dose because doing so may increase the risk of excessive blood pressure reduction. Abrupt discontinuation is generally not associated with rebound hypertension, but treatment should only be stopped under medical supervision to ensure continued cardiovascular protection and appropriate replacement therapy if necessary.

SIDE EFFECTS

Most patients tolerate amlodipine well, and many individuals experience no significant adverse effects during treatment. The most common mild side effect is swelling of the ankles or feet, known as peripheral edema, which occurs because blood vessels widen and allow fluid to accumulate in surrounding tissues. Facial flushing, a sensation of warmth, mild dizziness, and headache may occur, particularly during the early stages of treatment as the body adjusts to lower blood pressure. Some patients report fatigue, drowsiness, or a general feeling of tiredness, although these symptoms often improve over time. Mild nausea, abdominal discomfort, or indigestion can occur but are usually temporary and do not require discontinuation of therapy. Palpitations or an awareness of the heartbeat may occasionally be experienced due to reflex responses associated with blood vessel dilation. Moderate side effects may include more persistent swelling, significant dizziness, lightheadedness upon standing, or symptomatic low blood pressure, especially in individuals taking multiple blood pressure medications. Some patients may develop gum enlargement with prolonged therapy, emphasizing the importance of maintaining good oral hygiene and regular dental care. Rarely, liver enzyme elevations or abnormalities in liver function tests have been reported. Severe allergic reactions, including swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat accompanied by difficulty breathing, are uncommon but require immediate emergency medical attention. Rare cardiovascular complications such as worsening chest pain, new onset angina, or heart attack have been reported shortly after initiating therapy or increasing the dose, particularly in patients with severe underlying coronary artery disease. Extremely rare but serious reactions include severe hypotension, fainting, irregular heartbeat, jaundice, or severe skin reactions. Patients who experience persistent or worsening side effects should promptly consult their healthcare provider so that appropriate evaluation and possible treatment modification can be considered.

WARNINGS & PRECAUTIONS

Amlodipine should only be taken under the supervision of a qualified healthcare professional who can monitor blood pressure, treatment response, and overall cardiovascular health. Individuals with known hypersensitivity to amlodipine or any component of the formulation should not use the medication because serious allergic reactions may occur. Patients with severe low blood pressure, cardiogenic shock, or certain forms of advanced heart failure require careful medical evaluation before receiving this medication because excessive vasodilation may worsen their condition. Although amlodipine is generally considered safe in many patients with stable heart disease, initiation or dose escalation should be performed cautiously in individuals with severe obstructive coronary artery disease because temporary worsening of angina or myocardial infarction has rarely been reported. Since the medication is extensively metabolized by the liver, patients with hepatic impairment may require lower starting doses and slower dose adjustments to avoid excessive drug accumulation. Kidney impairment usually does not require routine dose adjustment because amlodipine is primarily metabolized by the liver, although overall clinical monitoring remains important in patients with significant renal disease. Alcohol may enhance the blood pressure lowering effects of amlodipine, increasing the risk of dizziness, fainting, or impaired coordination, so excessive alcohol consumption should be avoided. Patients should use caution when standing up quickly from sitting or lying positions because orthostatic symptoms may occur, especially during the beginning of treatment. Drug interactions may occur with medications that strongly affect liver enzymes responsible for amlodipine metabolism, including certain antifungal medications, antibiotics, antiviral agents, and anticonvulsants, making medication review essential before starting therapy. Grapefruit and grapefruit juice generally have less effect on amlodipine than on some other calcium channel blockers, but patients should still follow individualized advice from their healthcare provider regarding dietary interactions. During pregnancy, amlodipine should only be used when the expected benefits outweigh potential risks, and treatment decisions should be individualized by the prescribing clinician. Small amounts of amlodipine may pass into breast milk, so breastfeeding mothers should discuss the potential benefits and risks with their healthcare provider before continuing therapy. Patients should seek immediate emergency medical attention if they develop severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, fainting, sudden swelling of the face or throat, severe allergic reactions, or symptoms suggesting a heart attack or stroke, as these situations require urgent medical evaluation and treatment.
