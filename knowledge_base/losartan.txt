OVERVIEW

Losartan is a prescription medication that belongs to a class of drugs known as angiotensin II receptor blockers (ARBs). It is primarily used to treat high blood pressure and certain cardiovascular and kidney-related conditions by blocking the effects of angiotensin II, a naturally occurring hormone that causes blood vessels to narrow and stimulates the release of hormones that increase blood pressure. By preventing angiotensin II from binding to its receptors, losartan allows blood vessels to relax and widen, leading to improved blood flow and reduced blood pressure. This mechanism also decreases the workload on the heart and helps protect organs such as the kidneys from damage caused by prolonged hypertension. Losartan is considered a selective AT1 receptor antagonist, meaning it specifically blocks the receptor responsible for most of the harmful cardiovascular effects of angiotensin II while leaving other pathways relatively unaffected. It is available in oral tablet form and is commonly prescribed as a long-term maintenance medication rather than for immediate blood pressure reduction. After oral administration, losartan is absorbed through the gastrointestinal tract and is converted in the liver into an active metabolite that is even more potent than the parent drug. Both losartan and its active metabolite contribute to its therapeutic effects over a full day, allowing once-daily dosing for many patients. The medication is generally well tolerated and is associated with a lower incidence of persistent cough compared with angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors because it does not significantly affect bradykinin metabolism. Losartan has become a cornerstone therapy in the management of hypertension due to its proven effectiveness, favorable safety profile, and ability to reduce cardiovascular complications. It also provides kidney-protective benefits in specific groups of patients, particularly those with diabetes and proteinuria. The medication may be prescribed alone or in combination with other antihypertensive agents such as diuretics or calcium channel blockers when additional blood pressure control is needed. Consistent daily use is important because losartan controls blood pressure but does not permanently cure hypertension. Patients generally experience gradual improvements in blood pressure over several weeks, although some benefit may be observed within the first week of therapy. Long-term treatment with losartan has been shown to reduce the risk of stroke, slow the progression of kidney disease, and improve overall cardiovascular outcomes in appropriately selected patients.

INDICATIONS & USES

Losartan is primarily approved for the treatment of hypertension in adults and children of appropriate age to help lower elevated blood pressure and reduce the long-term risk of cardiovascular complications. Effective control of blood pressure decreases the likelihood of stroke, heart attack, heart failure, kidney damage, and other serious health conditions associated with chronic hypertension. Another major approved indication is the reduction of stroke risk in patients with hypertension who have left ventricular hypertrophy, a condition characterized by thickening of the heart muscle due to prolonged high blood pressure. Losartan is also approved to slow the progression of diabetic nephropathy in patients with type 2 diabetes who have hypertension and evidence of kidney disease, particularly those with proteinuria. By reducing pressure within the kidneys and decreasing protein leakage into the urine, the medication helps preserve kidney function over time. Physicians frequently prescribe losartan as part of combination therapy when blood pressure cannot be adequately controlled with a single medication. In clinical practice, losartan is commonly used off label for heart failure, particularly in patients who cannot tolerate angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors because of cough or angioedema. It may also be used for chronic kidney disease not related to diabetes when proteinuria is present, as reducing protein excretion may help slow disease progression. Some specialists prescribe losartan to lower elevated uric acid levels because it has mild uricosuric properties that promote the excretion of uric acid through the kidneys. This characteristic may provide additional benefit for certain patients with hypertension who also have gout or hyperuricemia. Losartan may also be considered in patients with metabolic syndrome because it has a relatively neutral effect on glucose and lipid metabolism compared with some older antihypertensive medications. It has been studied in patients with certain connective tissue disorders affecting the aorta, although this remains an off-label application and evidence continues to evolve. Healthcare providers determine whether losartan is appropriate based on an individual's blood pressure readings, underlying medical conditions, kidney function, cardiovascular risk, and concurrent medications. Although losartan effectively lowers blood pressure, it should be used alongside lifestyle modifications such as a healthy diet, regular physical activity, weight management, smoking cessation, and sodium restriction whenever appropriate. Continuous follow-up with healthcare providers helps ensure that treatment goals are achieved while minimizing potential adverse effects and complications.

DOSAGE & ADMINISTRATION

Losartan is administered orally and is available in tablet strengths that allow flexible dosing based on the patient's clinical condition and therapeutic response. The usual starting dose for hypertension in adults is 50 mg once daily, although some patients who are volume depleted or receiving high-dose diuretics may begin with 25 mg once daily to reduce the risk of excessive blood pressure lowering. Depending on blood pressure response, the dose may be increased gradually, with a common maintenance dose ranging from 25 mg to 100 mg daily. The total daily dose may be administered as a single daily dose or divided into two doses when clinically indicated. Patients with diabetic nephropathy often begin with 50 mg once daily, with the dose increased to 100 mg daily if tolerated and if additional therapeutic benefit is needed. In certain cases, elderly patients or individuals with liver impairment may require lower initial doses due to altered drug metabolism and increased sensitivity to treatment. Losartan may be taken with or without food because food has minimal impact on its overall effectiveness. It is recommended that patients take the medication at approximately the same time each day to maintain stable blood concentrations and improve adherence to therapy. Tablets should be swallowed whole with water unless a healthcare provider recommends an alternative administration method. Blood pressure should be monitored regularly, particularly during the initial weeks of therapy and after dose adjustments, to evaluate effectiveness and detect excessive hypotension. Kidney function and serum potassium levels should also be monitored periodically, especially in patients with kidney disease, diabetes, heart failure, or those taking medications that affect potassium balance. If a dose is missed, it should be taken as soon as the patient remembers unless it is nearly time for the next scheduled dose. If the next dose is approaching, the missed dose should be skipped and the regular dosing schedule resumed without taking a double dose. Abrupt discontinuation without medical advice is generally discouraged because blood pressure may rise again, increasing the risk of cardiovascular complications. Patients should continue taking losartan even if they feel well, as hypertension often produces no noticeable symptoms while still causing long-term damage.

SIDE EFFECTS

Losartan is generally well tolerated, and many patients take it for years without experiencing significant adverse effects. Common mild side effects include dizziness, particularly when standing up quickly due to a temporary drop in blood pressure, especially during the first few days of treatment. Fatigue, mild headache, nasal congestion, back pain, muscle aches, and occasional gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea or diarrhea may also occur. Some individuals may experience lightheadedness after the first few doses, particularly if they are dehydrated or taking diuretics. These mild symptoms often improve as the body adjusts to the medication. Moderate side effects can include symptomatic hypotension with fainting, persistent weakness, elevated potassium levels, or worsening kidney function in susceptible individuals. High potassium levels may cause muscle weakness, numbness, tingling, or irregular heart rhythms and require laboratory monitoring in patients at increased risk. In some patients, increases in blood creatinine or blood urea nitrogen may be observed, particularly in those with underlying kidney disease or narrowing of the renal arteries. Allergic reactions are uncommon but may include rash, itching, or swelling. Rare but serious side effects require immediate medical evaluation and may include angioedema involving swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat, which can interfere with breathing and become life threatening. Severe allergic reactions with difficulty breathing, widespread rash, or sudden swelling also require emergency treatment. Marked decreases in blood pressure resulting in confusion, loss of consciousness, or shock are rare but possible, especially in volume-depleted patients. Significant kidney injury may develop in susceptible individuals and can present with decreased urine output, swelling, or rapidly worsening kidney function. Very high potassium levels can lead to dangerous cardiac arrhythmias that require urgent medical attention. Although liver injury is uncommon, symptoms such as yellowing of the skin or eyes, dark urine, severe fatigue, or persistent abdominal pain should prompt immediate evaluation. Patients should promptly report any unusual or persistent symptoms to their healthcare provider so that appropriate adjustments or investigations can be performed.

WARNINGS & PRECAUTIONS

Losartan carries an important warning regarding its use during pregnancy because medications that affect the renin-angiotensin system can cause serious fetal harm or death when taken during the second and third trimesters of pregnancy. Women who become pregnant while taking losartan should contact their healthcare provider immediately so that an alternative treatment can be considered. The medication is generally avoided during pregnancy unless no safer alternatives are available under exceptional circumstances. Breastfeeding considerations should be discussed with a healthcare provider because limited information is available regarding the passage of losartan into human breast milk and potential effects on nursing infants. Patients with a history of hypersensitivity to losartan or any of its ingredients should not use the medication. Individuals with severe narrowing of both renal arteries or severe kidney impairment require careful monitoring because the medication may worsen kidney function in certain circumstances. Patients with liver disease may have reduced metabolism of losartan and may require lower doses and closer supervision. Kidney function and potassium levels should be assessed before and during treatment, particularly in patients with diabetes, chronic kidney disease, heart failure, or those taking potassium supplements, potassium-sparing diuretics, or other medications that increase potassium levels. Alcohol may enhance the blood pressure-lowering effects of losartan, increasing the risk of dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting, so patients should use caution when consuming alcoholic beverages. Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, including ibuprofen and naproxen, may reduce the antihypertensive effect of losartan and increase the risk of kidney injury, especially in older adults or those with preexisting kidney disease. Combining losartan with other medications that affect the renin-angiotensin system, such as angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors or aliskiren in certain patients, may increase the risk of kidney dysfunction, hypotension, and hyperkalemia and should generally be avoided unless specifically recommended by a specialist. Patients should maintain adequate hydration, particularly during episodes of vomiting, diarrhea, or excessive sweating, because dehydration may increase the risk of symptomatic hypotension and kidney injury. Before surgery or major medical procedures, patients should inform healthcare professionals that they are taking losartan. Immediate emergency medical attention should be sought if symptoms such as difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat, severe chest pain, loss of consciousness, profound weakness, severe allergic reactions, or signs of dangerously high potassium such as severe muscle weakness or irregular heartbeat occur. Regular medical follow-up, adherence to prescribed therapy, and routine laboratory monitoring help maximize the benefits of losartan while minimizing the risk of serious complications.
